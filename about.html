Our Story

Rooted in River Falls

A look at how we grow

Clifton Valley Farms began with a simple dream: to bring locally-grown, high-quality blooms and plants to the River Falls community and surrounding businesses. Founded in 2023, we are a family-run operation dedicated to hands-on growing, sustainable practices, and friendly service.

We believe that healthy plants start with a healthy environment, which is why every bloom in our greenhouse is tended to with meticulous care and responsible cultivation methods. When you purchase a plant from us, you're not just buying a product; you're supporting a local family and bringing a little piece of our passion into your home or business.

Our commitment extends beyond our greenhouse walls. We strive to be an accessible resource for all gardeners, offering simple plant care advice and a welcoming atmosphere at our seasonal roadside stand.

Our Core Values

What drives the way we farm

Quality First

We guarantee the health and vitality of every plant we grow. From seed to shelf, our focus is on producing robust, beautiful greenery that lasts.

Community Focus

We are proudly local and dedicated to supporting our neighbors, whether through our seasonal stand or partnerships with local businesses.

Sustainable Growth

Our farming practices prioritize the environment, using responsible methods to ensure the health of our plants, our land, and our future.

Meet the Founder, Anna

Owner and Head Grower

Anna’s lifelong love for gardening led her to establish Clifton Valley Farms. What started as a hobby greenhouse quickly grew into a commercial operation driven by the desire to share her carefully cultivated plants with a wider audience.

With a degree in Horticulture and years of experience, Anna oversees every aspect of the farm, ensuring the Clifton Valley standard of excellence is met. Her favorite part of the job? Seeing a customer's face light up when they find the perfect plant.

When she’s not in the greenhouse, Anna enjoys spending time with her family on the property, tending to her personal garden, and exploring the natural beauty of Western Wisconsin.
